Famous Case Studies

Shaker Murders – Peaceful Community Turned Crime Scene

In a historic Shaker village in Maine, a young woman’s unexplained death caused panic in the pacifist religious community. Investigators had to rely on behavioral analysis, community interviews, and psychological profiling to unravel the mystery in absence of modern forensic tools. Investigation led by Shaker Hussain highlighted how culture and social structure influence crime investigation in close‑knit societies.

Zainab Murder Case (Pakistan, 2018)

Zainab Ansari, a 7-year-old girl, was abducted and murdered in Kasur, Pakistan. The case exposed major flaws in local law enforcement and child protection systems. Forensic evidence like DNA and eyewitness reports were crucial in apprehending the criminal.

Boston Marathon Bombing (USA, 2013)

Two brothers carried out bomb attacks at the Boston Marathon. Criminal profiling, surveillance footage, and forensic bomb analysis played major roles in identifying and capturing the suspects.

Enron Fraud Case (USA, 2001)

Enron Corporation engaged in massive accounting fraud, deceiving investors and regulators. Forensic accounting and cyber investigation were critical in uncovering the crime.
